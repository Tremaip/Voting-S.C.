Voting Absentee in South Carolina

Written By: Tremain Prioleau II

October 5, 2020

Image source: ramseycounty

With the Coronavirus pandemic changing life for everyone this year, true concerns were made about the dangers to voter's health in the general election. Because of this, voting by mail has become a popular choice among American's looking to participate in the democratic process while also keeping safe from COVID. The State of South Carolina is no different in this cause as it has allowed all South Carolina voters to vote by mail this year.

There are a few important things to understand as a South Carolina voter about the process of voting by mail or "absentee voting". The main areas to focus on are the laws that allowed this change, the past requirements that have been waived, and the process of receiving a ballot in the mail.

3 Important Things to Know

All South Carolina voters can vote by mail in 2020 because of the waived restrictions by the state government. When requesting a ballot, S.C. voters should select “State of Emergency” as their excuse. There is not “no excuse absentee voting” in S.C. so it is important that voters select “state of emergency” because it refers to the ongoing pandemic.

Voters can print out an absentee ballot application by visiting their county website or they can email or call their local county voter registration office and get a ballot mailed to them that way. Ballots will be sent out to voters starting at the beginning of October. Applications must be returned to county registration offices by 5 p.m on October 24th.

The past requirement of needing a witness signature has been waived for S.C. voters. However, this requirement has gone back and forth within state courts so it is important that voters either check on the status of witness signatures or they get a signature anyway in order to be safe.
